Personal SWOT Analysis

Focus on the shortcommings and you'll overlook the strengths

Strengths: What do you do best? What are your positive traits?

Creative Thinking;
I grew up in a naturally artistic family and the trait didn't skip out on me, because I enjoy drawing a lot in my spare time. This trait often gives me an authentic point of view and I find myself brainstorming on uncommon ideas.

To Anticipate;
I relatively find it easy to find connections based on context. In other words I am quick to find a way to compromise and thus to solve problems.
Maths has always been a strong subject of mine and although it's not necessary for ICT, it's a clear example that I'm good at problem solving. Another is a different hobby of mine being sailing. Being on a small sailboat, you need some quick thinking for every aspect of it.

To Adjust;
I've come to know at my part-time job that I'm easy to adjust to my surroundings and work, since, working at a garden centre and its florist shop, I've been put on various tasks and multiple at a time, succeeding and finishing them.

Being Assertive at times in my case means taking initiative and listening to others attentively. It's showing when I'm working on projects which I enjoy.

Furthermore, I am quite critical when it comes to judging my own work and if asked of that of others.
I'm also a pretty fast learner, which means when it's shown it doesn't take me much practice to understand and do.

Weaknesses: What personality traits may be holding you back in your studies/career?

Because I'm pretty Assertive, I don't have much difficulty showing my vulnerable side when I have to.

However, this makes me straight forward to some people and over the years has made me into an anxious person rather than very social. Nevertheless, I am an open book and sum it down to being a socially awkward social butterfly.

Speaking in front of a crowd is thus a hard thing for me to do. Being a critical thinker can make me overthink my ability to perform to the point I'm telling myself it has to be perfect.

Being a perfectionist is my biggest weakness at this point in my life if I may say so. This comes down to me taking criticism to heart and so it takes some time for me to see past it and learn from my mistakes. Often I force myself to do it again but perfectly and never wrong again, but I know that's not what time and learning is about.

Opportunities: How can you turn your strengths/weaknesses into opportunities?

In my eighteen years of living, most of my weaknesses have become strengths for me already, or I've come to terms with them.
The opportunities I see laying beyond are learning to take criticism well and being less nervous while speaking to a crowd. It's not that I'm bad at them at all, I'd just like to improve regarding them.

Threats: What obstacles do you face to succeed as a student? Could any of your weaknesses prevent you from succeeding in your studies/career?

The obvious threat is that I'm going to put too much pressure on myself, because I don't want to make mistakes and will do my best to avoid making them.

However, what I've learned is that making mistakes is fine as long as you learn from them. And I'd like to be remembered even when I do eventually make mistakes.
Even though I'm very aware of my own take on this, I still see it as a threat which I'll be grateful to stop being scared of.
