Three Interesting Companies

Google

Googlers work in teams in business strategy, design, finance, engineering and technology, marketing and communications, and sales.

Google uses 216 technology products and services including HTML5, Google Analytics, and jQuery. The IT technology Google is actively using are three technologies for its website. These include HSTS, GStatic Google Static Content, and QUIC.

Google's pace of hiring was also torrid in the second quarter of 2022 as the company added 10,000 new employees to its 163,906 workforce, up 17 percent year over year. For the rest of 2022, however, Google will focus hiring on engineering, technical and other crucial roles.

They're looking for engineers who bring fresh ideas from all areas, including distributed computing, large-scale system design, networking and data storage, security, artificial intelligence, UI design and mobile; the list goes on and is growing every day. They want their engineers to be versatile, display leadership qualities and be enthusiastic to take on new problems across the full-stack as we continue to push technology forward.
Google has their global headquarters in California and has more than 70 offices in 50 countries.

NOS

While NOS is a Dutch television program, it combines my interest of writing, as a journalist and creating film media. Television broadcast companies need IT specialists and programmers on the dayly, not only to work with the filming but also for their websites and other media.

Since the NOS is only located in the Netherlands, the amount of people working there is just above 600.
Working at the biggest news broadcast company in the Netherlands, means there are many possibilities to work there, but as a programmer it's rather logical to work in the film industry side or the webmedia side of NOS.

Nintendo

Nintendo has headquearters around the globe up until twelve different countries. Nintendo had a total number of 6,574 employees in its 2021 fiscal year.

Nintendo Platform Technology Development is Nintendo's Hardware division. It was formed in 2015 with Company President Tatsumi Kimishima combining Nintendo Integrated Research and Development with Nintendo System Development.

Nintendo Software Design & Development has developed numerous products for the Wii and Nintendo DS ranging from the Wii channels to Photo Dojo on DSiWare.

Nintendo Software Planning and Development's main purpose was to assist other developers in creating Nintendo published video games, though they themselves also develop original titles. They were consolidated into Nintendo EPD with EAD.
It's possible to work in these groups as an IT specialist or programmer.

The Nintendo Switch system software (also known by its codename Horizon) is an updatable firmware and operating system used by the Nintendo Switch video game console. It is based on a proprietary microkernel.

Nintendo nurtures their relationship-focused culture with compassion, sincerity, humility and honest communication.
