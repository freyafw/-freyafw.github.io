Motivation

What is my Motivation?

I've chosen to study ICT at HZ University with the main reason being, gaining enough study points, in hopefully the first year. Because then I'll have the choice to go study English Language and Culture at the University of Utrecht.

Nevertheless, I do take a lot of interest in programming, coding, digital design, game develepment and the English Language. The latter being why I, as a native Dutch speaker, preferred to be placed in the international class at HZ.

First of all, I choose this course to learn the basics of programming for a first year ICT-student. And second of all, because of the block focussing on game development.

However, having started my first year, I wonder how the other blocks will be, since my interest has been piqued. After a gap year, I have been ready as ever to start studying again. This clearly proves a part of my motivation being just learning.

Why ICT?

I need to start my dream somewhere. And if it isn't at the beginning of the end, it shall be from zero to one.

I applied to ICT with the main thought being, that I need to start somewhere. In the end I want to create something beautiful, inside and out, something timeless.

I gain some of my happiness from solving problems and achieving progress. Society evolves and methods improve, this means there will always be something new to learn and for me to be happy doing what I love.

With ICT I do not only start the journey towards my dream job, which truth be told is still unclear, I also learn the basics. Learning is a process of trial and error, which starts somewhere and will inevitably end. But until then, I want to keep creating.

What makes my Motivation?

According to Dan Pink's 'The puzzle of motivation'.

  • Autonomy: (The urge to direct our own lives) I can do every aspect of my project myself;
  • Mastery: Society evolves and methods improve, this means there will always be something new to learn;
  • Purpose: To make something that is timeless.
